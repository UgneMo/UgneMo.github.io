Hi, I’m Ugnė Aleksandra Morkutė, a PhD researcher in Human-Centered Technology at NYU Tandon School of Engineering.

My research focuses on human–AI interaction, trust in automated vehicles, and how people make sense of intelligent systems in complex, real-world environments.

I use methods such as virtual reality simulations, eye-tracking, and behavioral analysis to study how humans interact with emerging technologies. My work particularly explores how trust is formed, negotiated, and disrupted in high-stakes and socially dynamic contexts.

Currently, I am working on projects investigating group dynamics in automated vehicles and the design of human-centered AI systems. I am also interested in how interaction design can support transparency, trust, and effective collaboration between humans and intelligent systems.

More broadly, I am interested in designing technologies that are not only functional, but also socially aware, interpretable, and aligned with human needs.
